About Me

I am a Physics Ph.D. candidate at Harvard University specializing in high energy theory. My advisor is Xi Yin. I've been working on conformal field theory and string theory. My recent work focuses on formalization of mathematical physics through Lean.

Ongoing Research

Tactile Data for Retargeting in Imitation Learning

This project integrates tactile sensing into the imitation learning retargeting process by matching contact force and contact shape between human fingertip sensors and a robotic gripper to verify successful object grasping. The approach addresses retargeting failures in imitation learning by ensuring physically consistent contact during manipulation, improving robustness and task transfer.

Anomalous Dimension For Multi-trace Operators in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM

The project aims to compute tree-level anomalous dimensions of multi-trace operators in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Super Yang–Mills theory using a Hamiltonian perturbation approach. This approach is intended to simplify calculations, clarify multi-trace operator mixing, and provide a more transparent understanding of the large-N spectrum beyond current correlator-based limits.
